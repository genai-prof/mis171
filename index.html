Relative vs Absolute References

This is one of the most important (and most confused!) Excel concepts. Let's make it click.

⚠️ Most common beginner mistake: Copying a formula without locking the right cells — causing all your answers to go wrong!
📍 The $ Sign Explained
A2
Relative Reference
Moves with the formula when copied. Like giving directions: "Go 2 blocks right."
$B$4
Absolute Reference
Locked — never moves. Like a GPS coordinate: "Always go to 217 King St."
$B4 or B$4
Mixed Reference
One part locked, one part free. $B4 locks column B; B$4 locks row 4.
